About the platform

The Beck Depression Inventory Platform is meant to support patients and psychologists throughout the therapy, where the Beck Depression Inventory (BDI-II) is used as a psychometric test for measuring the severity of depression. This platform enables patients to complete the BDI their device of choice and submits it directly to the corresponding therapist, who will is able to see the evaluation in a chronological context.

About the Beck Depression Inventory

The Beck Depression Inventory, created by Aaron T. Beck, is a 21-question multiple-choice self-report inventory, one of the most widely used psychometric tests for measuring the severity of depression. Its development marked a shift among mental health professionals, who had until then, viewed depression from a psychodynamic perspective, instead of it being rooted in the patient's own thoughts. In its current version, the BDI-II is designed for individuals aged 13 and over, and is composed of items relating to symptoms of depression such as hopelessness and irritability, cognitions such as guilt or feelings of being punished, as well as physical symptoms such as fatigue, weight loss, and lack of interest in sex.

Participants are asked to rate how they have been feeling for the past two weeks. Each answer gets scored on a scale value of 0 to 3. Higher total scores indicate more severe depressive symptoms. The standardized cutoffs used are:

  • 0–13: minimal depression
  • 14–19: mild depression
  • 20–28: moderate depression
  • 29–63: severe depression
